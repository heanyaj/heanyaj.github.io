About

Science, language & the ocean

I'm a multilingual marine scientist from El Salvador, currently based in Matosinhos, Portugal. My work sits at the intersection of shark ecology, ocean policy, and community well-being — shaped by years of fieldwork across Taiwan, the Azores, and Latin America, and by a conviction that science only matters when it reaches the people who need it most.

My MSc research developed a standardized image-based identification methodology for blue sharks in the Mid-Atlantic — a cost-effective approach to long-term monitoring that bridges fieldwork and conservation practice. Beyond the lab, I focus on the social dimensions of ocean governance: who benefits, who's left out, and how early-career scientists from the Global South can lead the next generation of ocean solutions.

I work in four languages — Spanish, English, Mandarin Chinese, and Portuguese — which is less a credential than it is a worldview: that inclusive science requires meeting people where they are, in the language they think in. Whether at a UN panel, a regional mentorship seminar, or a national newspaper, the goal is the same — making the ocean matter to more people.
